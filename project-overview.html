Context & Problem

Each year, more than 600,000 Americans leave prison with hopes of rebuilding their lives, but the path to reintegration proves devastating: nearly two-thirds find themselves jobless after a year, and four in ten return to prison within three years. This revolving door of incarceration not only drains public resources but also fractures families and weakens communities.

Target Users

Individuals who have been recently released from prison and those looking to mentor and guide them through their reintegration process, supporting continued rehabilitation.
